Add shapes

The stamp design journey commences with the selection of a shape. Rest assured, the chosen shape can always be changed or deleted during the stamp making process.To add a shape simply click on shapes followed by the shape of your choice. It's so easy to make a stamp with SyampJam!

Note: For the purpose of this tutorial, we'll choose a Circle shape, envisioning the creation of a round company stamp.

Edit Shapes

After adding a circle, let’s go ahead and edit it as per our requirement. We have provided 3 editing techniques for shapes:

  1. Size: Controls the size of your shape. By dragging the slider you can increase or decrease it's size.
  2. Stroke: Controls the thickness of the shape, drag right to increase and left to decrease its thickness.
  3. Line-Break: This editing technique cuts the solid outline of the shape into smaller pieces. By dragging the slider you can control the length of each piece. Drag right to reduce the length of each piece or drag left to bring it back to a solid outline.

Pro Tip: By using a combination of multiple circles (shapes), each with a different size, stroke-width & line-break you can achieve numerous variations of your custom logo stamp.Get creative! Remember making a stamp is fun. Our stamp maker is made to be super fun!

The 3 editing techniques above apply to ALL 15 shapes. The line shape has 3 additional editing techniques associated with it:

  1. Horizontal Position: CDragging the horizontal position slider right moves the Line to the right of the canvas. Dragging it left moves it to the left.
  2. Vertical position: Just like horizontal positional control, you can also control the vertical position. Drag the slider left to move the line up. Dragging it right will move the line down.
  3. Rotation: TDrag the slider to rotate the line around its axis. We have provided a complete 360 degree rotation. Moving the slider right will rotate the line clockwise. Moving it left makes it rotate counterclockwise.

Note: Positional control is only applicable to the Line shape and Line text (we will talk more about the line text in the next section)

Add Text

TSince we have envisioned a round stamp, we will add a “Circular text” in this step to complement the circle you added in the first step. Click on text followed by the circular text button. The chosen text will be painted on the canvas.

Pro Tip: Hover over the different text types after clicking “Text” on the left of the screen. While you hover, you will notice a “Tool-tip” describing each text type.

Important: In order to design a stamp it is very important to add at least one text element to your custom stamp design through which you can enter business, company, address and registration information.

After adding a text to your stamp design (circular, line, rectangular etc) it is mandatory to enter your custom text in the text box provided. If you don’t, the application will not let you download your design and will give you a “Enter Text” notification. See image below.

Edit Text

WAfter adding a circular text, you will see its corresponding edit box on the right side of the screen. The edit box has a plethora of editing techniques. We will walk through each one so you get comfortable with making a stamp online with StampJam.

  1. Text: Enter your desired text into this field.
  2. Font: Choose a font from the list. We have 50 fonts to choose from. The chosen font is applied to the chosen text.
  3. Style: We have included 5 text styling techniques: This editing technique cuts the solid outline of the shape into
    • Bold: Makes your text bold
    • talic: Makes your text italic
    • Uppercase: Converts your text into Capital letters
    • Invert: Changes your text from clockwise to counter-clockwise (see image below)
    • Whiten: Whitens your text (we will dive deeper into how whitening text enhances your stamp design.
  4. Font Size: Drag the slider to increase or decrease the font size. We have limited the minimum font size to 24 as we want the text to be visible once the stamp design has been inserted into a document. Anything smaller and the text in your custom stamp would be difficult to read.
  5. Letter Spacing: This text editing technique increases or decreases the space between each letter in your text. The default spacing has been set to 100 which is the maximum spacing. To decrease the spacing between each letter, drag the slider left.
  6. Radius: Drag the slider right to increase the radius of the text. Drag it left to decrease it.
  7. Rotation: By dragging the rotation slider we can choose the starting point for our text. The default position has been set to 0, with a maximum of 100. This allows us to position the text in the desired starting point. Drag the slider right to rotate the text clockwise, drag it left to rotate the text counter-clockwise in your stamp design.

Now that we have added a shape and a text, edited them according to our requirements, it’s time to understand how to highlight, edit on the go, hide and/or delete an element from your custom rubber stamp using the Layer section.

The layer section allows the user to choose a layer from the layer’s list in order to:

  1. Highlight the selected element on the canvas. While hovering your mouse over the layers list, the corresponding element will be highlighted on the canvas.
  2. By clicking on a particular layer, the corresponding edit box for the selected element will appear above. Now we can edit the chosen element from its corresponding edit box.
  3. Each item in the layer’s list has its own eye icon. By clicking on the eye, that particular element will be hidden in the canvas and the open eye will now appear closed. To unhide the element in the canvas, click on the closed eye and the layer will reappear on the canvas. This feature allows the user to view their online rubber stamp without certain elements by simply hiding them before they decide to delete or keep the element.
  4. To delete an element from the canvas, click on the element you want to delete from the layer’s list and then click delete in the “Tool-bar”. The element will now be deleted from the canvas as well the layers list.

Pro-Tip: The latest element added to the canvas will be on top of the layer list. Once you delete an element you can’t bring it back.

Danger Zone: If you are not satisfied with your stamp design and want to make your own stamp online from scratch, click on “Reset” from the tool-bar section > click “Yes, clear the canvas”. This will permanently delete the entire rubber stamp so that you can start the stamp design process again.

Add & edit images

Now that we have added shape(s) and text element(s), you can choose to add image(s) from our image library, or upload your own brand image.

As soon as you click on an image, the image will be drawn in the center of the canvas, the corresponding edit box will be generated and its entry into the layers section will occur simultaneously. We have provided 4 editing techniques for images:

  1. Size: By dragging the slider left or right you can decrease or increase the size of the image.
  2. Horizontal position: Drag the slider to position the image horizontally (left/right) on the canvas.
  3. Vertical position: Drag the slider to position the image vertically (up/down) on the canvas.
  4. Rotation: Drag the slider to rotate the image around its axis. We have provided a complete 360 degree rotation.

To upload an image, click on Images > “Upload Image” and choose the image you want in your stamp design.

Choose a color

Now that we have the building blocks to any custom stamp, it wouldn't be a personalized rubber stamp if we aren't allowed to change the color of the stamp design.

In order to change the color of the entire stamp, click on the “Fill Icon” from the “Tool-bar” section on top of the canvas. There are 4 ways to change the color:

  1. Manual: Drag the small circle inside the color palette towards your desired color. You can also directly click anywhere in the color palette.
  2. RGB: Enter the desired red, green and blue values in the boxes below.
  3. HSL: To enter a HSL value click on the R G B text to display the HSL input box. Now enter the desired values in the HSL input field.
  4. HEX: In order to enter a HEX value, click on R G B text to display the HEX input box. Now enter the desired HEX code.

Working with templates

We have professionally designed 100’s of pre-made stamp templates for your convenience. You can always choose to start the stamp design process with pre-made templates.

Step 1: Choose a shape

Even if you want to use a pre-made template, our tool is designed to always start with a shape. It doesn't matter which shape you choose if you intend to use a template, the shape can always be changed/deleted later during the stamp making process.

Step 2: Choose a template

Click on “Templates” from the left side of the screen. The desired template will be painted to the canvas, saving you time and effort.
Now you are free to edit the template’s text, shapes and images the same way you would if you were designing a stamp from scratch.

Pro-tip: The different elements in the template will be added to the layers section for you to edit as per your requirements.

Download your stamp

Once you are satisfied with your stamp design, it is time to receive your custom rubber stamp.

Click on “Download” in the top right of the page > enter your email address > click “Submit”.

Wait a few seconds while our system processes your request. Don’t forget to leave us feedback on the next page.

Check your email for your stamp design. The email will have a zip folder attached and the contents of the zip folder are mentioned below:

  1. Stamp.png: the original file that you designed using our tool.
  2. Bonus PNG’s: your original stamp is also email in 4 additional colors at no extra charge
  3. Old_style.png: This image will add a worn/old look to your personalized rubber stamp.
  4. Support.txt: This text file contains important information regarding your stamp design. Your unique product id will be mentioned in the text file with which you can contact us at the mentioned email address if you have any questions.

Aaaaand that’s a wrap! You are now fully equipped to design your free stamp in under 5 minutes using our stamps maker.

We hope you had as much fun designing your own stamp as we did teaching you how to!
